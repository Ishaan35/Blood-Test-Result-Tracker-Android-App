

• Albumin and globulin are two types of protein in your body. The total protein test measures the total amount albumin and globulin in your body. It’s used as part of your routine health checkup. It may also be used if you have unexpected weight loss, fatigue, or the symptoms of a kidney or liver disease.<br>
<br>

• Proteins are important building blocks of all cells and tissues. Proteins are necessary for your body’s growth, development, and health. Blood contains albumin and globulin. Albumin proteins keep fluid from leaking out of your blood vessels. Globulin proteins play an important role in your immune system.<br>
<br>
A total protein test is completed as part of your routine health checkup. It’s one of the tests that make up your comprehensive medical panel (CMP). It may be ordered if you have:<br>
<br>
<br>

• unexplained weight loss <br>
• fatigue <br>
• edema, which is swelling caused by extra fluid in your tissues <br>
• symptoms of kidney or liver disease <br>
<br>
<br>

<b> Normal Ranges: </b>
<br>
    • The normal range is 6.0 to 8.3 grams per deciliter (g/dL) or 60 to 83 g/L.
<br>
<br>


<b> Deficiency</b> can cause the following symptoms: <br>
<br>
    • fatigue and weakness <br>
    • recurrent viral or bacterial infections<br>
    • thinning, breaking hair<br>
    • hair that falls out<br>
    • brittle nails and dry skin<br>
    • mood changes and irritability<br>
    • cravings for protein-rich foods<br>
