
• Alanine aminotransferase (ALT) is an enzyme found mostly in the liver.
<br>
<br>
• ALT is also found in kidneys, heart, and the pancreas, in smaller measures.
<br>
<br>
<br>

• The purpose of the acid phosphatase test is to  <br>
 • diagnose liver diseases such as currhoses and hepatitis <br>
 • diagnose liver damage <br>
 • Find out if jaundice caused in a person is a result of liver disease or blood-related disorders <br>

<br>
<br>
• Some medicines and herbal supplements may need to be stopped, so talk to doctors prior to taking the test.
<br>
<br>
• ALT is measured if liver damage is suspected. Low levels of ALT are normal, however, if liver damage occurs, ALT is released into the bloodstream.

<br>
<br>
<br>
<b> Normal Range: </b>
<br>
    • 29-33 units per liter U/L for males <br>
    • 19-25 units per liter U/L for females
