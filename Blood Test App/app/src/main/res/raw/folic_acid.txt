
• A folic acid test measures the amount of folic acid in the blood. Folic acid is one of many B vitamins. The body needs folic acid to make red blood cells (RBC), white blood cells (WBC), and platelets, and for normal growth. Folic acid also is important for the normal development of a baby (fetus).
<br>
<br>

• Folate-deficiency anemia is the lack of folic acid in the blood. Folic acid is a B vitamin that helps your body make red blood cells. If you don't have enough red blood cells, you have anemia.
<br>
<br>

<br>
<br>
<b> Normal Range: </b>
<br>
    • 2.7 to 17.0 nanograms per milliliter (ng/mL)

<br>
<br>

<b>Decreased </b> levels can cause the following symptoms:
<br>
<br>

• extreme tiredness (fatigue) <br>
• lack of energy (lethargy)<br>
• breathlessness<br>
• feeling faint<br>
• headaches<br>
• pale skin<br>
• noticeable heartbeats (palpitations)<br>
• hearing sounds coming from inside the body, rather than from an outside source (tinnitus)<br>
• loss of appetite and weight loss<br>
