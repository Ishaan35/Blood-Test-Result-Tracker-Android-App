
• Ascorbic acid (Vitamin C) is a powerful anti-oxidant and a reducing agent.
<br>
<br>
• Ascorbic acid has several <b>body functions</b> which include: <br> <br>
    • Formations of neuropeptides<br>
    • Growth and repair of tissues in all parts of your body<br>
    • Formation of collagen<br>
    • Repair and maintain cartilage, bones, and teeth<br>
    • Aid in the absorption of iron<br>
    • Heal wounds and form scar tissue<br>
    • Detoxifying enzymes in the liver<br>

<br>
<br>
<br>

• The test is ordered when  <br>
 • Vitamin C deficiency is suspected <br>
 • Excess intake of vitamin C is suspected <br>

<br>
<br>
<br>
<b> Normal Range: </b>
<br>
    • The reference range of vitamin C is 0.6-2 mg/dL
<br>
<br>

Vitamin C <b>deficiency</b> may indicate: <br><br>
• Scurvy - A disease that results from the lack of Vitamin C. Early symptoms of deficiency include weakness, feeling tired and sore arms and legs. Without treatment, decreased red blood cells, gum disease, changes to hair, and bleeding from the skin may occur.
