
• Aspartate aminotransferase (AST) is an enzyme found in cells throughout the body but mostly in the heart and liver.
<br>
<br>
• ALT is also found in kidneys and muscles, in smaller measures.
<br>
<br>
<br>

• The purpose of the acid phosphatase test is to  <br>
 • To detect liver damage and/or to help diagnose liver disease <br>
 • To monitor liver function in those taking certain medications <br>

<br>
<br>
• This test is carried out to detect any liver damage.

<br>
<br>
<br>
<b> Normal Range: </b>
<br>
    • Males: 10-40 units/L (U/L). <br>
    • Females: 9-32 units/L (U/L).

<br>
<br>

<b>Increased </b> levels can indicate:
<br>
<br>
 • Acute viral hepatitis (10 times greater than normal values) <br>
 • Chronic hepatitis (less than 4 times greater than normal values) <br>
 • Cirrhosis <br>
