
• Biotin is a vitamin which is part of the Vitamin B family, and is also known as Vitamin H. It can be found in protein rich foods, however, it can be taken in the form of supplements.
<br>
<br>
• Biotin is needed for several parts of the body to function which include nerves, blood cells, bone marrow, and sweat glands. It also plays a vital role in fat, protein, and carbohydrate metabolism.
<br>
<br>
<br>

• The purpose of the acid phosphatase test is to  <br>
 • To detect liver damage and/or to help diagnose liver disease <br>
 • To monitor liver function in those taking certain medications <br>

<br>
<br>
• This test is ordered when someone is suspected of having biotin deficiency. However, biotin deficiency is very rare. Fatigue and hair loss are some symptoms.

<br>
<br>
<br>
<b> Normal Range: </b>
<br>
    • 200-1200 ng/L

<br>
<br>

<b>Decreased </b> levels can cause the following side effects:
<br>
<br>
 • Hair Loss <br>
 • Fatigue <br>
 • Depression <br>
 • Muscle pain <br>
 • Weakened Immune System <br>

<br>
<br>

<b>Deficiency is caused by:
<br>
<br>
 • Malnutrition <br>
 • Chrohn's disease <br>
 • Depression <br>
